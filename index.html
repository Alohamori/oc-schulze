using the Schulze method

Cells with values contain the strength of the strongest path (by average margin of victory) from the team on the left to the team at the top.
You can hover over these to see the details of the pairing. The teams are sorted by their Schulze results (number of teams against whom they have a stronger path), but you can click the headings to sort alphabetically or by the number of real tournament rounds survived.
The tables are truncated to exclude teams with no positive result, but these can be revealed by expanding the table.
Finally, the meanings of the colors are as follows:
and
indicate "standard" wins and losses, where actual margin of victory is sufficient to determine the pairwise result.
represents a theoretical win; the teams never played, but the path from A to B is stronger than the (usually nonexistent) path from B to A.
denotes situations wherein a team's result was improved by Schulze, but not by enough to win the pairing.
and
are reserved for the six cases in which a team lost an actual match to a Schulze-weaker one.
can be read as an implicit zero; the teams never played and there exists no theoretical path to victory.
